04. Engineering Blueprint

Behind the Scenes (Code)

Logic for the attendance engine:

Time Converter

We use a calcHours() function that takes strings like "09:00" and "17:30", subtracts any break minutes, and converts the final result into decimal hours (like 8.25).

Overtime Engine

The system is programmed with a STANDARD_HOURS of 8. Any minute worked beyond this threshold is automatically flagged as "Overtime" and added to your weekly bonus pool.

Real-Time Timers

We use a Date.now() calculation to show the live timer. It subtracts the totalBreakMins in real-time, so you always see your "Effective Work Time" correctly.

Memoized Stats

We use useMemo for the summary tables. This ensures that even with 300 employees and months of history, the data updates instantly without slowing down the app.

// Converting strings to decimal hours
const [inH, inM] = clockIn.split(":").map(Number);

05. How to use this page

Starting your work day? Here’s the best way to leverage Time Tracking:

STEP 1: CLOCK IN EARLY

Click the **Clock In** button as soon as you start your work. This ensures every minute of your contribution is recorded accurately for payroll.

STEP 2: LOG YOUR BREAKS

Heading for lunch? Take the coffee icon for a spin! Toggling the **Break** button pauses the timer so your totals only reflect "Deep Work."

STEP 3: REVIEW THE WEEK

On Friday afternoon, check the **Weekly Summary**. If you worked late on Tuesday, make sure the red **OT Badge** is visible on the daily log!
