04. Engineering Blueprint

Behind the Scenes (Code)

Logic for the financial settlement:

Currency Formatter

We use a custom fmt() utility with .toLocaleString(). It automatically adds the dollar sign and commas (like $10,000) for you.

Summing Engine

To find the Total Net Pay, we use .reduce(). It loops through the entire payroll list and adds up the final numbers in one second.

Visible Filter

We use hasAccess() to decide what data to fetch. If you aren't HR, the system only asks the database for **your** ID.

Visual Indicators

We use text-destructive for deductions and text-[hsl(142,72%,40%)] for bonuses to make the table read like a bank statement.

// Summing the visible data (Total Net)
const totalNet = visibleData.reduce((sum, p) => sum + p.netPay, 0);
05. How to use this page

Reviewing your salary? Here’s the best way to leverage Payroll:

STEP 1: CHECK NET PAY

Your **Net Pay** is your final take-home salary. This is what will appear in your bank account at the end of the month.

STEP 2: REVIEW TAXES

Look at the **Deductions** column (in red). This shows how much money was directed toward taxes, insurance, or pension plans.

STEP 3: CELEBRATE BONUSES

If you see a green number in the **Bonus** column, congratulations! This is extra reward money added to your base salary.
